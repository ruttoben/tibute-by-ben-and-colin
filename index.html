Nelson Mandela

  • 1918 - Born in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1944 - Helped found the African National Congress Youth Leagu
  • 1948 - The National Party came to power in South Africa and began implementing apartheid, a system of institutionalized racial segregation.
  • 1952 -Led the Defiance Campaign, a non-violent civil disobedience campaign against apartheid laws.
  • 1964 - Sentenced to life imprisonment for his activism, spending the next 27 years in prison, including 18 years on Robben Island.
  • 1985 - Initiated secret talks with the South African government to end apartheid and negotiate for democratic elections.
  • 1993 - Shared the Nobel Peace Prize with South African President F.W. de Klerk for their efforts to end apartheid and establish a democratic South Africa.
  • 1994 - Elected as the first black President of South Africa, leading the country through a peaceful transition to democracy and promoting national unity through his policy of reconciliation.
  • 1995 - Hosted the Rugby World Cup, using the sport to promote reconciliation between black and white South Africans.
  • 2001 - Established the Nelson Mandela Foundation, which promotes his vision of a just and equitable society.
  • 2014 - Passed away at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy as a champion of human rights and an inspiration to people around the world.

"No one is born hating another person because of the color of his skin, or his background, or his religion. People must learn to hate, and if they can learn to hate, they can be taught to love, for love comes more naturally to the human heart than its opposite." This quote exemplifies his commitment to promoting unity and reconciliation, even in the face of immense adversity and injustice.

-- President Nelson Mandela
